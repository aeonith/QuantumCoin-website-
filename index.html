What QuantumCoin™ Is

QuantumCoin™ (QC) is a sound-money, Proof-of-Work Layer-1 that launches already insulated against quantum-era cryptography shocks. QC preserves what made Bitcoin historic— fair distribution, simple rules, no premine—and upgrades the signatures to post-quantum schemes so value today isn’t compromised by the hardware breakthroughs of tomorrow.

The promise is straightforward: a chain you can run, audit, and hold for decades. Predictable issuance (2-year halvings, ~66-year horizon) meets an owner-local safety circuit (RevStop™) that gives holders last-resort control if keys are threatened— without introducing a central authority.

  • Investor clarity: hard cap of 22,000,000 QC; no hidden allocations.
  • Security moat: post-quantum signatures remove “upgrade-under-duress” risk.
  • Durability: PoW issuance, client diversity, and documentation designed for decades.

Founder’s Letter — From Breach to Blueprint

Markets reward the networks that survive. I built QuantumCoin™ after watching quantum research outpace the plans most chains had for “fixing it later.” That gap—between the pace of innovation and the pace of governance—creates a simple risk: when quantum breaks legacy signatures, holders will be asked to trust emergency upgrades under fire.

QC is my answer: prepare now. Keep issuance honest with Proof-of-Work. Keep ownership honest with post-quantum signatures. Give users a personal safety lever with RevStop™ so if keys are targeted, the owner—not a committee—can pull a cryptographic brake on their coins while the network continues moving.

For investors, the thesis is discipline and timing:

  • Timing tailwind: quantum capability is a when-not-if event; QC is built for that regime today.
  • Moat via simplicity: minimal rule-set, transparent policy, and credibility through refusal to “print.”
  • Adoption path: pruned/light nodes widen participation; institutional-grade docs and stable APIs invite builders.

I didn’t design QC to be the loudest. I designed it to be the last one standing.

The Quantum Threat (Why This Matters)

  • Signature forgeries: scalable quantum computers can break legacy signatures.
  • Harvest-Now-Exploit-Later: attackers archive today’s data to forge transactions later.
  • Longevity: money needs cryptography that lasts decades, not product cycles.

QC launches with post-quantum signatures so we don’t need an emergency fork later.

Design Choices

Post-Quantum Signatures

Transactions use PQ schemes designed to resist key-recovery and signature forgery by quantum computers.

Proof-of-Work

Open participation and fair distribution. ~10-minute blocks keep validation predictable.

RevStop™

Owner-local, optional “circuit breaker.” If keys are compromised, the owner can pause spends of their UTXOs.

Broad Nodes

Archival for explorers, pruned for low storage, and light/SPV for phones—to maximize decentralization.

RevStop™ (Owner-Local Protection)

  • Local, not global: affects only your UTXOs; the protocol verifies your authority.
  • Opt-in: wallets can enable/disable; no central admin exists.
  • Forensics-friendly: on-chain signals distinguish a self-protective pause from seizure.

Mining

Mining secures the chain and distributes new QC. Difficulty targets ~10-minute blocks. Hardware choice is open—run what you have, optimize if you want. The policy discourages surprises: predictable halvings, no stealth inflation, and validation code that favors readability.

Running a Node

Archival

Full history for explorers and research — highest storage, best for analytics & infra providers.

Pruned

Validates the tip with low storage by discarding old blocks while keeping full security.

Light/SPV

Header-based proofs for mobile devices — verify payments without full blocks.

Governance & Upgrades

QC favors conservative governance. Consensus rules change only with broad community support and exhaustive review. Client diversity and reproducible builds are prioritized to minimize correlated risk.

FAQ

Is QuantumCoin™ a fork?

No. QC is an independent PoW chain designed around post-quantum signatures and a simple monetary policy.

How do RevStop™ and decentralization coexist?

RevStop™ is local and cryptographically authorized by the wallet owner; it cannot pause others’ funds or the chain.

How do I participate?

Run a pruned node, mine, contribute to code/docs, or build apps on top of the explorer & API.
